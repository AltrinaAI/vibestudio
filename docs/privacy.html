Privacy Policy

Effective July 10, 2026 · Applies to the VibeStudio desktop (macOS) and iOS apps

The short version: VibeStudio does not collect any data about you. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no tracking. Your devices talk directly to your own computers — nothing passes through servers we operate, because we don't operate any.

What we collect

Nothing. The apps have no accounts to sign up for, contain no analytics or tracking code, and do not collect or transmit any personal data, usage data, or content to us or to anyone on our behalf.

SSH keys

The iOS app generates SSH key pairs on your device. The private key is stored in the iOS Keychain and never leaves your device — it is not backed up to us, synced through us, or visible to us in any way. The desktop app likewise keeps any secrets it manages in the macOS Keychain. Only the public key is ever shown to you, so you can install it on your own computers.

Where your data goes

When you use VibeStudio, connections go directly from your device to computers you own and control, over SSH. Your code, terminal sessions, skills, and agent conversations travel only between your own devices. We have no server in the middle and no access to any of it.

Downloads from GitHub

The first time the app connects to one of your computers, it downloads its small server component from GitHub Releases. That download is an ordinary HTTPS request to GitHub, so GitHub's standard server logs apply (for example, the IP address making the request). That request is covered by GitHub's privacy statement, not ours — we receive nothing from it.

Crash reports

The only diagnostics we can ever see come through Apple's own opt-in mechanisms: TestFlight feedback and the App Store's "Share with App Developers" crash reporting. Those are controlled entirely by your settings with Apple, and what we receive is what Apple provides — anonymized crash logs, if you have opted in.

Children

Because the apps collect no data at all, this applies equally to everyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the apps ever change in a way that affects your privacy, we will update this page and change the effective date at the top.
