Support

What you need

VibeStudio runs coding agents on your own computer — a Mac or Linux machine with SSH enabled that your phone can reach (same network, a VPN like Tailscale, or a machine with a public address). The app does not provide computers; it connects to yours.

Getting started on iOS

  1. Add a connection. Tap + and enter your computer's host (name or IP), port (usually 22), and your username on that machine.
  2. Generate a key. VibeStudio creates an SSH key pair on your phone. The private key stays in the iOS Keychain; you only ever handle the public key.
  3. Install the public key. Copy the public key and append it to ~/.ssh/authorized_keys on your computer, for the user you entered in step 1. For example, on the computer: 
    echo 'PASTE_PUBLIC_KEY_HERE' >> ~/.ssh/authorized_keys
  4. Save the connection.
  5. Connect. VibeStudio signs in over SSH, sets up its server on the machine, and you're ready to work.

Frequently asked questions

I can't connect at all.

Check the basics first: is the host, port, and username exactly right? Is the public key installed (step 3 above)? Can your phone actually reach the machine — are they on the same network, or connected through something like Tailscale? On a Mac, also confirm SSH is on: System Settings → General → Sharing → Remote Login.

It says authentication was rejected.

The connection reached the machine, but the key isn't accepted — almost always because the public key isn't in ~/.ssh/authorized_keys for that user. Double-check you added it under the same username you entered in the app, and that the file wasn't mangled when pasting (each key is a single line).

What runs on my computer?

A small server that VibeStudio installs into ~/.vibestudio. It runs only while you're connected and exits when you disconnect. You can remove it any time by deleting that folder.

Does my code pass through your servers?

No. Your phone connects directly to your computer over SSH. There is no relay, no cloud, and no account — see the privacy policy.
